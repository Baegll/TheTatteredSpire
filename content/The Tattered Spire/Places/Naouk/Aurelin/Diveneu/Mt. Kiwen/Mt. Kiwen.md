# Introduction

Known as the "Mountain of Promises", [[Mt. Kiwen]] is revered by those who seek the blessings of [[Velthara]], [[Dealine]], and [[Silviana]].

During certain times in the [[Yehr]], those standing upon [[The Palemuth Bridge]] may gaze toward [[Mt. Kiwen]] at night and witness rare alignments of the moons: [[Alvorden]], [[Neton]], and [[Zeaf]]. Most of the time, only two moons partially overlap, creating a modest spectacle. On rare occasions, all three moons intersect in a partial alignment. Rarest of all is the moment when they fully overlap — a sight considered nothing short of miraculous.

Devotees of [[Silviana]] seek deep meaning in these lunar convergences. The specific combination of intersecting moons is only one facet among many in the intricate art of celestial divination. Every alignment is studied in context — position, duration, and the phase of each moon — forming a complex web of omens and interpretations.

---
# Geography
The upper half of [[Mt. Kiwen]] was once composed primarily of a soft, black stone susceptible to erosion. Over time, wind and rain has worn down this surface layer, exposing harder rock beneath. This process created natural terraces of black sand along the slopes — formations the locals call “beaches.”

# Traditions
#### [[Palestone]] Mania
In certain beaches, shimmering grey flecks of [[Palestone]] can be seen glinting against the dark sands. Though their presence doesn’t signal a true deposit, many opportunist sift through the sands in search of the precious stone. Whenever a sizable [[Palestone]] is discovered, a [[Palestone]] Mania ensues. The most famous being the [[Palestone Mania of 180]].

#### Lovers' Binding
Many lovers choose to propose on [[The Palemuth Bridge]]. Beyond its natural symbolism of connection, the bridge’s viewpoint with the intersecting moons makes it an auspicious place to exchange vows.

For the most lovestruck, the journey continues with a pilgrimage to top of [[Mt. Kiwen]]. There, beneath the open sky and on black beaches, couples write their names on slips of paper and release them into the wind. It is said that [[Dealine]] gathers these names and inscribes them into a celestial registry, enshrining their union.


# Introduction

During certain times in the [[Yehr]], those who stand on the [[The Palemuth Bridge]] can look towards [[Mt. Kiwen]] at night and see a combination of intersections of the moons ([[Alvorden]], [[Neton]], [[Zeaf]]). Most of the time these events are not noteworthy, as usually only two moons partially overlap one another. However, it is relatively rare for all three to partially intersect and exceptionally rare when they all fully overlap.

Many believers in [[Silviana]] try to find auspicious meaning behind each combination of moons. Some say the intersection of [[Alvorden]] and [[Zeaf]] is good luck but a full overlap is bad luck.
